B. Streaming Dataflow — what persists vs what resets

chunk t = 1 → t = 2 → t = 3 → … → t = T
r(D,H̄) ↑ picked once per video at L5     Mt EMA momentum (per-chunk)     St TAST state (cross-chunk)     KVt LLM cache (cross-chunk, sliding)

Three persistent state streams cross chunk boundaries. The rate r is chosen once per video; the per-chunk path (L1–L7) is repeated T times. Kept-token positions reset every chunk.

E. Theoretical framing — rate–distortion trade-off

Pruning at rate (1−r) introduces distortion d(r) in the LLM's visual posterior. Short/medium video has redundant per-chunk content → d(r=0.30) is small; long-form video has dense per-chunk plot evidence → d(r=0.30) is large (≈ 7.5 pp loss).

The adaptive selector L5 chooses r to satisfy d(r) ≤ ε per video while minimising tokens. With ε ≈ 2 pp of vanilla, the empirical Pareto recovers:
